# Systematic Passage-by-Passage Comparison: T250 vs T251 vs T223

## Purpose

This document extends the epithet observation (Attwood 2017b) into a systematic pattern. For each segment of the Heart Sutra, we compare T250 and T251 against T223 (Kumarajiva's Large Prajnaparamita) to determine whether T250 consistently reproduces T223 more closely than T251 does.

---

## Methodology

- **Base text:** T251 (Xuanzang, ca. 649-656 CE) as analytical reference
- **Comparison text:** T250 (attributed Kumarajiva, date uncertain)
- **Source text:** T223 (Kumarajiva, 404 CE) -- the Large Prajnaparamita from which both texts extract
- **Comparison categories:**
  - **Terminology:** Which text uses T223's terminology?
  - **Content:** Which text includes/omits passages found in T223?
  - **Phrasing:** Which text follows T223's syntax more closely?

## Segment-by-Segment Analysis

### 1. Opening (T250:1 / T251:1)

| Feature | T223 (Kumarajiva) | T250 | T251 | Closer to T223 |
|---------|-------------------|------|------|-----------------|
| Avalokitesvara | 觀世音 | 觀世音 | 觀自在 | **T250** |
| Skandha term | 五陰 | 五陰 | 五蘊 | **T250** |
| "all empty" | 空 | 五陰空 | 五蘊皆空 | **T250** (simpler, closer to source) |
| Prajnaparamita | 般若波羅蜜 | 般若波羅蜜 | 般若波羅蜜多 | **T250** (lacks 多) |

**Summary:** T250 preserves Kumarajiva's terminology in all four features. T251 updates all four to Xuanzang's conventions.

### 2. Skandha Characteristics (T250:2a / T251: ABSENT)

| Feature | T223 (Kumarajiva) | T250 | T251 | Closer to T223 |
|---------|-------------------|------|------|-----------------|
| Passage present? | Yes (expanded) | Yes | **Absent** | **T250** |
| Pattern: "X空故無Y相" | Present in Large Sutra | Present | — | **T250** |

**T250 text:** 色空故無惱壞相，受空故無受相，想空故無知相，行空故無作相，識空故無覺相。何以故？

**Analysis:** This passage, which attributes a specific empty characteristic to each of the five skandhas, has a clear parallel in the Large Prajnaparamita's discussion of emptiness. T250 preserves it; T251 omits it entirely. Under the revision hypothesis, a reviser would have had to deliberately delete coherent doctrinal elaboration -- a practice undocumented in the 抄經 tradition (see CHAOJING_REVISION_PRACTICES.md).

### 3. Form-Emptiness (T250:2b / T251:2)

| Feature | T223 (Kumarajiva) | T250 | T251 | Closer to T223 |
|---------|-------------------|------|------|-----------------|
| Negation formula | 非色異空 | 非色異空 | 色不異空 | **T250** |
| "also like this" | 亦如是 | 亦如是 | 亦復如是 | **T250** |
| Sariputra name | 舍利弗 | 舍利弗 | 舍利子 | **T250** |

**Analysis:** T250's 非色異空 ("it is not that form differs from emptiness") uses the emphatic negation pattern characteristic of Kumarajiva's translations. T251's 色不異空 ("form does not differ from emptiness") is a more standard Chinese construction. Both convey the same meaning, but T250's syntax is closer to Kumarajiva's style.

### 9. Mantra Praise -- Epithets (T250:11 / T251:11)

| Feature | T223 (Kumarajiva) | T250 | T251 | Closer to T223 |
|---------|-------------------|------|------|-----------------|
| Number of epithets | 3 | **3** | 4 | **T250** |
| Epithet style | 明呪 throughout | 明呪 throughout | 大神咒/大明咒/無上咒/無等等咒 | **T250** |
| Pattern consistency | Consistent (all vidya) | Consistent (all 明呪) | Mixed (神咒 + 咒) | **T250** |

**T250:** 是大明呪，是無上明呪，是無等等明呪
**T251:** 是大神咒，是大明咒，是無上咒，是無等等咒

**Analysis:** This is the strongest single piece of evidence, already documented by Attwood (2017b). T250 preserves the Large Sutra's three-epithet pattern with consistent 明呪 (vidya-mantra) terminology. T251 adds a fourth epithet (大神咒) and drops 明 from three of the four, creating an inconsistent pattern. The four-epithet pattern in T251 has no precedent in the Prajnaparamita literature and appears to be an innovation.

## Pattern Conclusion

The comparison reveals a **systematic and consistent pattern**, not a single observation:

1. **Terminology:** In every case where T250 and T251 differ in terminology, T250 uses Kumarajiva's (T223) conventions and T251 uses Xuanzang's conventions. There is no exception.

2. **Content:** Two entire passages present in T250 but absent from T251 (skandha characteristics, temporal negation) have parallels in the Large Prajnaparamita. T251 has no passages absent from T250 that are found in T223.

3. **Phrasing:** Where syntax differs (非色異空 vs 色不異空; 亦如是 vs 亦復如是), T250's phrasing is closer to Kumarajiva's translation style.

4. **The negation core is identical:** The five-section negation series (sections 4-8) is word-for-word identical in both texts, consistent with both independently extracting the same well-known passage.

This pattern is difficult to explain under a revision model (either direction) but natural under parallel composition: two compilers working independently from T223, one using Kumarajiva-era conventions and one using Xuanzang-era conventions, would produce exactly this pattern.

---

## Interpretive vs Alternative Explanations

### This pattern is compatible with:
- **Parallel composition** (both texts independently extract from T223)
- **T250 as earlier, T251 as update** (the traditional model) -- but this doesn't explain why T251 deleted two entire doctrinally correct passages

### This pattern is difficult to explain under:
- **T250 as later forgery restoring T223 material** (Attwood's model) -- a forger would need to systematically replace every Xuanzang-convention term with its Kumarajiva equivalent, add two complete passages, and maintain perfect T223 consistency throughout. This is possible but requires a forger with unusual textual sophistication.

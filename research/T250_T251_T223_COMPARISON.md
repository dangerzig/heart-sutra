# Systematic Comparison: T250, T251, and T223 (Large Sūtra)

## Passage-by-Passage Extraction Fidelity Analysis

This table documents how T250 and T251 each relate to Kumārajīva's T223 (大般若波羅蜜經, 404 CE), the identified source for the Heart Sūtra's core content.

---

## Methodology

For each Heart Sūtra passage, we compare:
1. Does T250 match T223 exactly (verbatim extraction)?
2. Does T251 match T223 exactly?
3. If not, what is the nature of the transformation?

**Categories:**
- **VERBATIM** = Exact character-for-character match with T223
- **ADAPTED** = Same meaning, different wording
- **INNOVATED** = New formulation not in T223
- **EXPANDED** = Additional content beyond T223
- **CONDENSED** = Abbreviated from T223

## Conclusion

The systematic comparison reveals a clear pattern:

1. **T250 reproduces T223 verbatim** in most passages, especially:
   - Avalokiteśvara name (觀世音)
   - Skandha term (五陰)
   - Śāriputra name (舍利弗)
   - "Not different" formula (非色異空)
   - **Epithet formula (3 epithets with 明呪)** — exact character-for-character match

2. **T251 consistently updates or innovates**:
   - New Avalokiteśvara name (觀自在)
   - New skandha term (五蘊)
   - New Śāriputra form (舍利子)
   - Rephrased "not different" (色不異空)
   - **New epithet formula (4 epithets, drops 明)**

3. **T250 has material absent from T251**:
   - Skandha-characteristic elaboration
   - Temporal negation
   - Additional phrase in bodhisattva section (苦惱)

This pattern is **inconsistent with T251 being a revision of T250**. A reviser would:
- Keep terminology (not systematically replace it)
- Keep doctrinal elaborations (not delete them)
- Keep source-text correspondences (not obscure them)

The pattern **is consistent with parallel composition**: both T250 and T251 independently extracted from T223, making different compositional choices.

# Complete Textual Collation

## Prajñāpāramitāhṛdaya: All Witnesses Compared

---

## Collation Methodology

This document presents a segment-by-segment collation of all principal witnesses to the Heart Sūtra. Following the Chinese compositional priority thesis (Nattier 1992), T251 serves as the base text against which all other witnesses are compared.

**Witnesses Collated:**
- **T251**: Xuanzang's Xīnjīng (ca. 649-656 CE) — Base text
- **T250**: Dàmíngzhòujīng (pre-Xuanzang?) — Parallel Chinese composition
- **Sanskrit**: GRETIL edition with Hōryūji (Ja) variants
- **Tibetan**: Degé Kangyur (Toh 21) — Long recension

**Notation:**
- `+` = Addition (material present that is absent from base text)
- `−` = Omission (material absent that is present in base text)
- `~` = Variant reading (different wording for same content)
- `C→S` = Chinese to Sanskrit direction of dependence
- `S→T` = Sanskrit to Tibetan direction of dependence

## Segment 1: Opening (Avalokiteśvara's Observation)

### T251 (Base Text)
> 觀自在菩薩，行深般若波羅蜜多時，照見五蘊皆空，度一切苦厄。

### T250
> 觀世音菩薩，行深般若波羅蜜時，照見五陰空，度一切苦厄。

| Location | T251 | T250 | Analysis |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| Avalokiteśvara | 觀自在 | 觀世音 | T250 uses older translation; T251 uses Xuanzang's new rendering |
| -pāramitā | 波羅蜜多 | 波羅蜜 | T250 lacks final 多 (duō) |
| Skandhas | 五蘊皆空 | 五陰空 | T250: older term 陰 (yīn); lacks 皆 (jiē, "all") |

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Ārya-avalokiteśvaro bodhisattvo gambhīrāṃ prajñāpāramitācaryāṃ caramāṇo vyavalokayati sma: pañca-skandhās, tāṃś ca svabhāva-śūnyān paśyati sma.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Chinese | Direction | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| pañca-skandhāḥ | Nominative | 五蘊 (acc. sense) | C→S error | Should be accusative pañcaskandhāṃs (Attwood 2015) |
| svabhāva-śūnyān | "inherently empty" | 皆空 | C→S expansion | Sanskrit adds philosophical precision |
| vyavalokayati sma | Imperfect tense | 照見 | C→S | Unusual tense for sūtra style |

### Tibetan (Toh 21)
> ཡང་དེའི་ཚེ་བྱང་ཆུབ་སེམས་དཔའ་སེམས་དཔའ་ཆེན་པོ་འཕགས་པ་སྤྱན་རས་གཟིགས་དབང་ཕྱུག་ཤེས་རབ་ཀྱི་ཕ་རོལ་ཏུ་ཕྱིན་པ་ཟབ་མོའི་སྤྱོད་པ་ཉིད་ལ་རྣམ་པར་བལྟ་ཞིང་། ཕུང་པོ་ལྔ་པོ་དེ་དག་ལ་ཡང་རང་བཞིན་གྱིས་སྟོང་པར་རྣམ་པར་བལྟའོ།

| Feature | Tibetan | Sanskrit | Direction | Notes |
|---------|---------|----------|-----------|-------|
| yang de'i tshe | "at that time" | — | S→T | Frame narrative marker (long recension) |
| sems dpa' chen po | "great being" | mahāsattva | S→T | Honorific expansion |
| rang bzhin gyis stong pa | "inherently empty" | svabhāva-śūnya | S→T | Matches Sanskrit exactly |

## Segment 2: Form and Emptiness

### T251 (Base Text)
> 舍利子，色不異空，空不異色；色即是空，空即是色。受想行識亦復如是。

### T250
> 舍利弗，非色異空，非空異色，色即是空，空即是色。受想行識亦如是。

| Location | T251 | T250 | Analysis |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| Śāriputra | 舍利子 | 舍利弗 | Different transliteration (both valid) |
| "not different" | 色不異空 | 非色異空 | T250: "it is not that form differs"; T251: "form does not differ" |
| "also like this" | 亦復如是 | 亦如是 | T250 lacks 復 |

### T250 Additional Passage (UNIQUE)
> 舍利弗，色空故無惱壞相，受空故無受相，想空故無知相，行空故無作相，識空故無覺相。何以故？

**Translation:** "Śāriputra, because form is empty there is no characteristic of affliction and decay; because feeling is empty there is no characteristic of feeling; because perception is empty there is no characteristic of cognizing; because volition is empty there is no characteristic of acting; because consciousness is empty there is no characteristic of awareness. Why is this?"

**Significance:** This passage is ABSENT from T251 and all Sanskrit/Tibetan witnesses. Its presence in T250 suggests either:
1. T250 preserves earlier material omitted by T251, OR
2. T250 independently elaborated on the skandha analysis

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Iha Śāriputra rūpaṃ śūnyatā śūnyataiva rūpam, rūpān na pṛthak śūnyatā śūnyatāyā na pṛthag rūpam, yad rūpaṃ sā śūnyatā yā śūnyatā tad rūpam; evam eva vedanā-saṃjñā-saṃskāra-vijñānāni.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Chinese | Direction | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| yad rūpaṃ sā śūnyatā... | Present | ABSENT | S+ interpolation | Line added in Sanskrit, not in any Chinese (Nattier 1992) |
| rūpān na pṛthak | Ablative | 色不異空 | C→S | Unusual ablative; calque from Chinese |

### Tibetan (Toh 21)
> གཟུགས་སྟོང་པའོ། སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་གཟུགས་སོ། གཟུགས་ལས་སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་གཞན་མ་ཡིན། སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་ལས་ཀྱང་གཟུགས་གཞན་མ་ཡིན་ནོ། དེ་བཞིན་དུ་ཚོར་བ་དང་། འདུ་ཤེས་དང་། འདུ་བྱེད་དང་། རྣམ་པར་ཤེས་པ་རྣམས་སྟོང་པའོ།

Tibetan follows Sanskrit structure but lacks the yad rūpaṃ line in some editions.

## Segment 3: Characteristics of Dharmas

### T251 (Base Text)
> 舍利子，是諸法空相，不生不滅，不垢不淨，不增不減。

### T250
> 舍利弗，是諸法空相，不生不滅，不垢不淨，不增不減。

**Variants:** Essentially identical except for 舍利子/舍利弗 (Śāriputra rendering).

### T250 Additional Passage (UNIQUE)
> 是空法，非過去，非未來，非現在。

**Translation:** "This emptiness-dharma is not past, not future, not present."

**Significance:** Temporal negation ABSENT from T251 and Sanskrit/Tibetan. Found in some Prajñāpāramitā sources.

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Iha Śāriputra sarva-dharmāḥ śūnyatā-lakṣaṇā, anutpannā aniruddhā, amalā avimalā, anūnā aparipūrṇāḥ.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Chinese | Direction | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| śūnyatā-lakṣaṇā | "having emptiness as characteristic" | 空相 | C→S | Matches |
| Six characteristics | 3 pairs | 3 pairs | — | Identical structure |

### Tibetan (Toh 21)
> ཤཱ་རིའི་བུ། དེ་ལྟར་ཆོས་ཐམས་ཅད་སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་དེ། མཚན་ཉིད་མེད་པ། མ་སྐྱེས་པ། མ་འགགས་པ། དྲི་མ་མེད་པ། དྲི་མ་དང་བྲལ་བ་མེད་པ། བྲི་བ་མེད་པ། གང་བ་མེད་པའོ།

Tibetan adds "mtshan nyid med pa" (characteristicless) not in Chinese or Sanskrit.

## Segments 4-6: Negation Series (Skandhas, Āyatanas, Dhātus)

### T251 (Base Text)
> 是故空中，無色，無受想行識，無眼耳鼻舌身意，無色聲香味觸法，無眼界，乃至無意識界。

### T250
> 是故空中，無色，無受想行識，無眼耳鼻舌身意，無色聲香味觸法，無眼界，乃至無意識界。

**Variants:** Identical in both Chinese versions.

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Tasmāc Chāriputra śūnyatāyāṃ na rūpaṃ na vedanā na saṃjñā na saṃskārāḥ na vijñānam. Na cakṣuḥ-śrotra-ghrāṇa-jihvā-kāya-manāṃsi. Na rūpa-śabda-gandha-rasa-spraṣṭavya-dharmāḥ. Na cakṣur-dhātur yāvan na manovijñāna-dhātuḥ.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Chinese | Direction | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| Compound structure | Bahuvrīhi compounds | Individual negations | C→S | Sanskrit restructures |
| yāvan | "up to" | 乃至 | C→S | Direct calque |

### Tibetan (Toh 21)
Follows Sanskrit structure precisely.

## Segment 7: Dependent Origination

### T251 (Base Text)
> 無無明，亦無無明盡，乃至無老死，亦無老死盡。

### T250
Identical to T251.

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Na-avidyā na-avidyā-kṣayo yāvan na jarā-maraṇaṃ na jarā-maraṇa-kṣayo.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Chinese | Direction | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| **kṣaya** | "destruction/exhaustion" | 盡 (jìn) | C→S ERROR | Should be nirodha "cessation" |

**Critical Finding:** The Sanskrit uses *kṣaya* (destruction) where all standard Prajñāpāramitā literature uses *nirodha* (cessation). The Chinese 盡 can mean either. A back-translator chose the wrong Sanskrit equivalent. This is key evidence for Chinese priority (Nattier 1992).

### Tibetan (Toh 21)
> མ་རིག་པ་མེད། མ་རིག་པ་ཟད་པ་མེད་པ་ནས། རྒ་ཤི་མེད། རྒ་ཤི་ཟད་པའི་བར་དུ་ཡང་མེད་དོ།

| Feature | Tibetan | Sanskrit | Direction | Notes |
|---------|---------|----------|-----------|-------|
| **zad pa** | "exhaustion" | kṣaya | S→T | Tibetan preserves Sanskrit error |

**Critical Finding:** Tibetan *zad pa* means "exhaustion" (= kṣaya), NOT "cessation" (= 'gog pa). This proves Tibetan was translated from the Sanskrit that already contained the kṣaya error.

## Segment 8: Four Noble Truths and Attainment

### T251 (Base Text)
> 無苦集滅道，無智亦無得，以無所得故。

### T250
Identical to T251.

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Na duḥkha-samudaya-nirodha-mārgā. Na jñānaṃ, na prāptir na-aprāptiḥ.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Chinese | Direction | Notes |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-------|
| **na-aprāptiḥ** | "no non-attainment" | ABSENT | S+ interpolation | Late addition (Huifeng 2014) |

### Hōryūji Manuscript (Ja)
> na jñānaṃ na prāptiḥ

**Critical Finding:** The Hōryūji manuscript (7th-8th c.) LACKS na-aprāptiḥ, matching the Chinese. The interpolation appears only in later Nepalese manuscripts and Conze's edition.

### Tibetan (Toh 21)
> སྡུག་བསྔལ་བ་དང་། ཀུན་འབྱུང་བ་དང་། འགོག་པ་དང་། ལམ་མེད། ཡེ་ཤེས་མེད། ཐོབ་པ་མེད། མ་ཐོབ་པ་ཡང་མེད་དོ།

Tibetan includes *ma thob pa yang med do* ("no non-attainment"), showing it was translated from a Sanskrit source that already had the interpolation.

## Segment 9: Bodhisattva Result

### T251 (Base Text)
> 菩提薩埵，依般若波羅蜜多故。心無罣礙，無罣礙故。無有恐怖，遠離顚倒夢想，究竟涅槃。

### T250
> 菩薩依般若波羅蜜故，心無罣礙，無罣礙故，無有恐怖，離一切顛倒夢想苦惱，究竟涅槃。

| Location | T251 | T250 | Analysis |
|----------|------|------|----------|
| Bodhisattva | 菩提薩埵 | 菩薩 | T251 full transliteration; T250 abbreviation |
| -pāramitā | 波羅蜜多 | 波羅蜜 | T250 lacks 多 |
| "separated from" | 遠離顚倒夢想 | 離一切顛倒夢想苦惱 | T250 adds 一切 and 苦惱 |

### Sanskrit (GRETIL/Conze)
> Tasmāc Chāriputra aprāptitvād bodhisattvasya prajñāpāramitām āśritya viharaty acittāvaraṇaḥ. Cittāvaraṇa-nāstitvād atrastro viparyāsa-atikrānto niṣṭhā-nirvāṇa-prāptaḥ.

| Feature | Sanskrit | Notes |
|---------|----------|-------|
| niṣṭhā-nirvāṇa-prāptaḥ | Conze's reading | ERROR: should be niṣṭhānirvāṇaḥ (Attwood 2018) |

### Hōryūji Manuscript (Ja)
> niṣṭhānirvāṇaḥ

**Critical Finding:** Hōryūji has *niṣṭhānirvāṇaḥ* (bahuvrīhi compound: "one whose nirvāṇa is complete"), not Conze's *niṣṭhā-nirvāṇa-prāptaḥ*. The Chinese 究竟涅槃 supports the Hōryūji reading.

## Segment 11: Mantra Praise (Epithets)

### T251 (Base Text)
> 故知般若波羅蜜多，是大神咒，是大明咒，是無上咒，是無等等咒，能除一切苦，眞實不虛故。

### T250
> 故知般若波羅蜜，是大明呪，是無上明呪，是無等等明呪，能除一切苦，真實不虛。

| Feature | T251 | T250 | Analysis |
|---------|------|------|----------|
| Number of epithets | **4** | **3** | Major structural difference |
| Epithets | 大神咒、大明咒、無上咒、無等等咒 | 大明呪、無上明呪、無等等明呪 | T250 uses 明呪 throughout; T251 adds 神咒 and drops 明 |
| Final particle | 故 | — | T251 adds 故 |

### Large Sūtra (T223) Comparison
> 是大明呪、無上明呪、無等等明呪

**Critical Finding:** T250's three epithets EXACTLY match the Large Sūtra (T223). T251 innovates with four epithets. The Sanskrit also has four epithets, matching T251 not T250. This suggests:
1. T250 preserves the original Large Sūtra pattern
2. T251 innovated with four epithets
3. Sanskrit was back-translated from T251, not T250

### Sanskrit (GRETIL)
> Tasmāj jñātavyam: prajñāpāramitā mahā-mantro mahā-vidyā-mantro 'nuttara-mantro 'samasama-mantraḥ, sarva-duḥkha-praśamanaḥ, satyam amithyatvāt.

Four epithets matching T251 structure.

### T250
> 故說般若波羅蜜呪，即說呪曰：竭帝竭帝，波羅竭帝，波羅僧竭帝，菩提僧莎呵。

### T250 Unique Material (Absent from T251)

1. **Skandha Characteristics Passage:**
   > 色空故無惱壞相，受空故無受相，想空故無知相，行空故無作相，識空故無覺相。

## Critical Findings Summary

### Evidence for Chinese Compositional Priority (C→S)

1. **kṣaya/nirodha error**: Sanskrit uses wrong term; Chinese 盡 is ambiguous
2. **Grammatical calques**: rūpān na pṛthak (ablative) from Chinese 色不異空
3. **T223 parallels**: Heart Sūtra matches Kumārajīva's translation specifically
4. **Dating**: Chinese witnesses precede Sanskrit manuscripts

### Evidence for T250/T251 as Parallel Compositions

1. **T250 has material absent from T251**: Skandha characteristics, temporal negation
2. **T250 preserves Large Sūtra patterns**: Three epithets match T223 exactly
3. **Internally consistent terminology**: Each text uses consistent translation conventions

### Late Interpolations Identified

1. **oṃ** in mantra (9th-10th c. Tantric addition)
2. **na-aprāptiḥ** (absent from Hōryūji and all early Chinese)
3. **Elaborate maṅgala** (late Nepalese only)
4. **Frame narrative** (long recension development)
5. **niṣṭhānirvāṇaprāptaḥ** (Conze's error; should be niṣṭhānirvāṇaḥ)

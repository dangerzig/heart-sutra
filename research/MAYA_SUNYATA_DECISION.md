# Māyā→Śūnyatā Observation: In or Out?

*Phase 5, TODO #9 — February 2026*

---

## The Observation

The Heart Sutra's "form is emptiness" formula (色即是空, *rūpaṃ śūnyatā*) appears to be a creative transformation of the Aṣṭasāhasrikā Prajñāpāramitā's "form is illusion" formula (*rūpam eva māyā māyaiva rūpam*).

The transformation: **māyā (illusion) → śūnyatā (emptiness)**

This is not a mechanical extraction but a deliberate theological reinterpretation.

## Does T250 Handle This Differently from T251?

**No.** Both T250 and T251 use the śūnyatā/空 (emptiness) formula, not the māyā/幻 (illusion) formula:

- **T251**: 色不異空，空不異色，色即是空，空即是色
- **T250**: 非色異空，非空異色，色即是空，空即是色

The syntactic structure differs slightly (色不異空 vs 非色異空), but both versions substitute śūnyatā for māyā identically. Neither preserves the original māyā formulation.

This means the māyā→śūnyatā transformation was already in place before the T250/T251 divergence. It occurred at the level of the original extraction from T223, not at the level of the T250/T251 split.

## Relevance to Parallel Composition Hypothesis

**Minimal.** The māyā→śūnyatā observation:

- Does **not** distinguish T250 from T251 (both make the same substitution)
- Does **not** support or weaken the parallel composition hypothesis specifically
- Does **not** depend on the Chinese-priority framework (though it is most naturally explained within it)

It is relevant to the broader question of the Heart Sutra's compositional character (digest text vs. creative recomposition) but not to the specific T250/T251 relationship that is the paper's focus.

## Recommendation: Remove from Paper, Save for Separate Publication

1. It does not support the core argument (parallel composition of T250 and T251)

## Decision

**Remove from this paper. Save for separate publication.**

The observation is genuine and interesting, but it does not advance the parallel composition argument. Including it would weaken the paper by dispersing focus.

# Comprehensive Survey: Major Mahayana Sutras with Multiple Chinese Translations

## Beyond the Original Ten Case Studies

---

## Purpose

This document extends the survey in `CHAOJING_REVISION_PRACTICES.md` (which documents ten cases) to cover ALL remaining major Mahayana sutras in the Taisho canon that exist in multiple Chinese translations. For each case, we assess whether the retranslation/revision involved deletion of doctrinally correct content from an earlier version.

**The ten cases already documented (NOT repeated here):**
1. Astasahasrika Prajnaparamita (T224 -> T227)
2. Pancavimsatisahasrika Prajnaparamita (T222 -> T223)
3. Vajracchedika / Diamond Sutra (T235 -> T220, six translations total)
4. Vimalakirtinirdesa (T474 -> T476, three translations)
5. Saddharmapundarika / Lotus Sutra (T263 -> T262)
6. Avatamsaka (T278 -> T279)
7. Mahaparinirvana (T374 -> T375)
8. Smaller Sukhavativyuha / Amitabha Sutra (T366 -> T367)
9. Larger Sukhavativyuha (T362 -> T360)
10. Suvarnaprabhasa / Golden Light Sutra (T663 -> T665)

## NEW CASE 11: Lankavatara Sutra (Lankavatarasutra)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Lankavatarasutra
- **Chinese:** 楞伽經 (Lengqie jing)
- **Taisho Nos.:** T670, T671, T672 (+ one lost translation by Dharmaraksa, early 5th c.)

### Translations

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date | Fascicles |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| T670 | 楞伽阿跋多羅寶經 | Gunabhadra (求那跋陀羅) | 443 CE | 4 |
| T671 | 入楞伽經 | Bodhiruci (菩提流支) | 513 CE | 10 |
| T672 | 大乘入楞伽經 | Siksananda (實叉難陀) | 700-704 CE | 7 |

### Direction of Change
- T670 (shortest, 4 fasc.): Lacks chapters 1, 9, and 10 found in later versions. This is the version transmitted from Bodhidharma to Huike in Chan tradition.
- T671 (longest, 10 fasc.): **Criticised in the imperial preface to T672** for containing "extra words and sentences mixed in that detract from the original meaning." The criticism is of **addition**, not deletion.
- T672 (7 fasc.): Most polished, complete, and philosophically refined; used five separate Sanskrit editions. Fazang assisted.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** The progression from T670 to T672 is consistently expansive. T670 is shorter because Gunabhadra's source manuscript was incomplete (missing chapters 1, 9, 10), not because later translators deleted content. The only documented criticism (of T671) concerns *interpolated additions*, not deletions. T672 aimed for accuracy and completeness, not abridgement.

## NEW CASE 12: Samdhinirmocana Sutra (Unraveling the Intent)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Samdhinirmocanasutra
- **Chinese:** 解深密經 (Jie shenmi jing)
- **Taisho Nos.:** T675, T676, T677, T678, T679

### Translations

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date | Scope |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|-------|
| T678 | 相續解脫地波羅蜜了義經 | Gunabhadra (求那跋陀羅) | 435-443 CE | Ch. 9 only |
| T679 | 相續解脫如來所作隨順處了義經 | Gunabhadra | 435-443 CE | Ch. 10 only |
| T675 | 深密解脫經 | Bodhiruci (菩提流支) | 514 CE | Prologue + 10 chapters (complete) |
| T677 | 佛說解節經 | Paramartha (真諦) | 557 CE | First 4 chapters only |
| T676 | 解深密經 | Xuanzang (玄奘) | 647 CE | Prologue + 8 chapters (complete) |

### Direction of Change
The earliest translations (T678/T679) are partial--only one chapter each. Bodhiruci (T675) produced the first complete version. Paramartha (T677) was partial (4 chapters). Xuanzang (T676) produced the most authoritative complete version with improved Yogacara terminology.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** The direction is from partial to complete. Later versions expand coverage and refine terminology. Xuanzang's T676 is considered the most reliable and complete. No content present in earlier versions was removed from later ones.

## NEW CASE 13: Dasabhumika Sutra (Ten Stages Sutra)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Dasabhumikasutra
- **Chinese:** 十住經 / 漸備一切智德經 / 十地經
- **Taisho Nos.:** T285, T286, T287 (+ incorporated in Avatamsaka T278/T279)

### Translations

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date | Notes |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|-------|

Progressive retranslation with updated terminology and increasing fidelity. The six known Chinese translations span from Dharmaraksa (3rd c.) to Siladharma (8th c.).

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** Each retranslation provides a fresh rendering with updated terminology and increased completeness. The direction is additive.

## NEW CASE 14: Srimaladevisirhhanada Sutra (Queen Srimala's Lion's Roar)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Srimaladevisirhhanadanirdesa
- **Chinese:** 勝鬘師子吼一乘大方便方廣經 (Shengman shizi hou...)
- **Taisho Nos.:** T353, T310(48)

### Translations

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|
| T353 | 勝鬘師子吼一乘大方便方廣經 | Gunabhadra (求那跋陀羅) | 435 CE |
| T310(48) | 勝鬘夫人會 (ch. 48 of Ratnakuta) | Bodhiruci (菩提流支) | 706-713 CE |

### Direction of Change
Gunabhadra's standalone version (T353) is the earlier and more widely studied. Bodhiruci's version was incorporated into his Ratnakuta compilation nearly three centuries later. Scholars use Gunabhadra's version as the base text precisely because it is earlier and shows the earlier state of Tathagatagarbha thought.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** The Bodhiruci version represents a retranslation with updated terminology, not an abridgement. Both versions are substantively complete.

## NEW CASE 15: Tathagatagarbha Sutra

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Tathagatagarbhasutra
- **Chinese:** 如來藏經 (Rulaizang jing)
- **Taisho Nos.:** T666, T667

### Translations

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date | Recension |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|-----------|
| T666 | 大方等如來藏經 | Buddhabhadra (佛陀跋陀羅) | c. 420 CE | Shorter |
| T667 | 大方廣如來藏經 | Amoghavajra (不空) | c. 750 CE | Longer |

### Direction of Change
Zimmermann (2002) identifies these as two distinct recensions: the shorter (T666, Buddhabhadra) and the "more extended and detailed" (T667, Amoghavajra). The later version is longer, not shorter.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** The later version expands upon the earlier. The direction of change is consistently additive.

### Full Translation History

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|

| T225 | 大明度經 | Zhi Qian (支謙) & Kang Senghui | 223-229 CE |
| T226 | 般若鈔經 | Dharmaraksa (竺法護) | 386 CE |

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** Each version reflects different source manuscripts and translation philosophies. Nattier has shown that Kumarajiva's apparent "abbreviations" correspond to readings in earlier Indic manuscripts, not deliberate cuts.

## NEW CASE 22: Mahayana Mahaparinirvana Sutra (Faxian vs. Dharmaksema)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Mahaparinirvanasutra
- **Chinese:** 大般泥洹經 / 大般涅槃經
- **Taisho Nos.:** T376, T374, T375

### Translations

| Taisho | Title | Translator | Date | Size |
|--------|-------|-----------|------|------|
| T376 | 大般泥洹經 | Buddhabhadra & Faxian (法顯) | 416-418 CE | 6 fascicles |
| T374 | 大般涅槃經 | Dharmaksema (曇無讖) | 421-432 CE | 40 fascicles |
| T375 | 大般涅槃經 (Southern Version) | Huiyan, Huiguan, Xie Lingyun et al. | 430s CE | 36 fascicles |

NOTE: T374/T375 is listed among the original ten. The Faxian version T376 is documented here as a distinct case requiring separate analysis.

### Direction of Change
T376 (Faxian, 6 fasc.) contains only the first five chapters, corresponding to the original Indian core. Dharmaksema's T374 (40 fasc.) is four times longer. The extra ~30 fascicles have no Sanskrit parallels and are likely of Central Asian origin. Crucially, **Dharmaksema also altered the icchantika passages** in the first five chapters (which overlap with T376) to bring them into line with the new doctrine of universal buddha-nature taught in the later chapters.

### Special Note: Doctrinal Alteration (Not Deletion)
This is the most important case to examine carefully. Dharmaksema *altered* icchantika passages in the overlapping core: where T376 (Faxian) states icchantikas are "bereft of buddha-nature and without hope for spiritual renewal," Dharmaksema's T374 revises this to allow for eventual enlightenment. This is **doctrinal harmonization**, not deletion. The revision changed the *doctrinal position* (from exclusion to inclusion of icchantikas) rather than deleting correct doctrine. Dharmaksema's alterations served to bring the earlier chapters into conformity with the expanded, pro-icchantika theology of the later chapters he was translating for the first time.

This case shows a reviser *changing* content but not *deleting* it. The motivating principle was doctrinal consistency, not abbreviation. Daosheng (c. 360-434) was famously exiled for rejecting the Faxian version's anti-icchantika stance and was later vindicated when Dharmaksema's fuller translation arrived.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No -- but with qualification.** Dharmaksema altered icchantika passages for doctrinal harmonization. However: (a) the alteration was to change doctrinal position, not to delete content; (b) the motivation was theological consistency, not abbreviation; (c) the overwhelming direction was massive expansion (6 -> 40 fasc.). This case is the closest thing to a counter-example in our survey but does not constitute deletion of *correct* doctrine -- Dharmaksema's changes reflected what he believed was the *more complete* teaching.

## NEW CASE 25: Maharatnakuta Collection (Structural Case)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Maharatnakuta
- **Chinese:** 大寶積經 (Da baoji jing)
- **Taisho No.:** T310 (+ T311-T373 as separate translations of component sutras)

### Structure
Bodhiruci compiled the Ratnakuta (T310) between 706 and 713 CE, assembling 49 "assemblies" (individual sutras). He used **23 texts already circulating in Chinese** (by earlier translators) and added **26 new translations of his own**. The earlier translations (T311-T373) survive independently in the Taisho alongside their Ratnakuta versions.

### Direction of Change
This is a compilation case, not a revision case. Bodhiruci preserved the earlier translations within the compilation framework. Where he retranslated, he produced fresh versions rather than editing earlier ones.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** The compilation model preserved older translations. Both the Ratnakuta versions and the separate older translations survive in the Taisho.

## NEW CASE 26: Mahasamnnipata Sutra (Great Assembly Sutra)

### Text Identity
- **Sanskrit:** Mahasamnnipatasutra
- **Chinese:** 大方等大集經 (Da fangdeng daji jing)
- **Taisho No.:** T397

### Translation History
A composite text translated by multiple translators over time:
- Fascicles I-XI and XIII: Dharmaksema (曇無讖), 414-421 CE
- Fascicle XII: Zhiyan and Baoyun, c. 427 CE (filling the missing Aksayamatinirdesa)
- Fascicles XIV-XVII: Narendrayasas

Earlier lost translations by Lokaksema (2nd c.) and Kumarajiva (early 5th c.) are no longer extant.

### Direction of Change
The extant version is a composite of multiple translators' work. Earlier complete translations are lost. The surviving text was assembled by filling gaps over time -- adding the missing Aksayamatinirdesa chapter (fasc. XII) that Dharmaksema's translation had lacked.

### Deletion of Doctrinally Correct Content?
**No.** The only documented editorial action was **addition** -- filling in a missing chapter. Lost translations cannot be compared.

## CRITICAL ASSESSMENT: The Lankavatara Bodhiruci Case

The Lankavatara case (T671) deserves special attention because it is the only case in our survey where a *specific criticism* of a translation's content is documented. The imperial preface to Siksananda's T672 criticises Bodhiruci's T671 for containing "extra words and sentences mixed in that detract from the original meaning."

This criticism documents **interpolation** (addition of extraneous content), not deletion. The imperial court's concern was that Bodhiruci *added too much*, not that he *removed* anything. This is consistent with the pattern documented throughout our survey: the recurrent problem in Chinese Buddhist translation was excessive addition, not deletion of correct doctrine.

## CRITICAL ASSESSMENT: The Mahaparinirvana Icchantika Case

The Faxian/Dharmaksema Mahaparinirvana case (T376/T374) is the most complex in our survey and the closest potential counter-example. Dharmaksema altered icchantika passages from the earlier core text to harmonize with the expanded theology of universal buddha-nature.

However, this case differs fundamentally from the T250/T251 Heart Sutra situation:

1. **Motivation:** Dharmaksema altered passages because the earlier version's icchantika doctrine was *contradicted* by the new chapters he was translating. The alteration served doctrinal consistency. By contrast, the T250 passages absent from T251 (skandha characteristics, temporal negation, 苦惱) are not contradicted by anything else in T251; they are doctrinally consistent additions.

2. **Nature of change:** Dharmaksema changed the *doctrinal position* on icchantikas (from hopeless to saveable). He did not delete passages while leaving the rest unchanged. By contrast, the revision hypothesis for T250/T251 would require deleting coherent passages while preserving the surrounding text intact.

3. **Scale:** Dharmaksema's overall direction was massive expansion (6 -> 40 fascicles). The icchantika changes are minor harmonization within a vast additive project. By contrast, T250 -> T251 would require *only* deletion, with no compensating expansion.

---

## OVERALL FINDING

### Across all cases surveyed (original ten + sixteen new cases = 26 total):

| Direction of Change | Count | Examples |
|--------------------|-------|----------|
| Expansion (new chapters, vows, content) | Very common | Sukhavativyuha (24->48 vows), Golden Light (13->31 ch.), Tathagatagarbha, Avatamsaka, Mahaparinirvana |
| Terminology update | Universal | Every case from Lokaksema through Xuanzang |
| Fresh retranslation from different source MS | Very common | Lankavatara, Vimalakirtinirdesa, Samdhinirmocana |
| Restoration of missing material | Documented | Lotus Sutra Devadatta ch., Mahasamnnipata gap-fill |
| Compilation preserving earlier translations | Documented | Ratnakuta (Bodhiruci), Mahasamnnipata |
| Doctrinal harmonization (changing position) | Rare | Mahaparinirvana icchantika passages (Dharmaksema) |
| **Deletion of doctrinally correct content** | **Not documented in any case** | -- |
| Interpolation criticized (excess addition) | Documented | Lankavatara T671 (Bodhiruci), Mahaparinirvana T374 (Dharmaksema) |

### The documented revision pattern in 26 cases spanning seven centuries (2nd-8th c. CE) is unambiguous:
- Revisers **add**, **update**, **expand**, **harmonize**, and **restore**.
- When content changes occur, the criticism is of **excess addition**, not deletion.
- **Deletion of doctrinally correct content from an earlier Chinese version is not documented in any case examined.**

---

## Implications for T250/T251

The T250 passages absent from T251 (skandha characteristics, temporal negation, 苦惱) are:
- Doctrinally correct (standard Prajnaparamita)
- Not contradicted by anything in T251
- Not terminologically obsolete
- Not sectarianly controversial

The revision hypothesis would require their deliberate deletion -- an action unprecedented in 26 cases spanning seven centuries. The parallel composition hypothesis explains their absence naturally: two independent compilers extracting from T223 made different selections.

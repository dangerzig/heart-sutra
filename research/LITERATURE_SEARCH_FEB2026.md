# Literature Search: Potentially Missing References (February 2026)

Two parallel searches conducted across both papers (parallel compositions and Tibetan edition). References below are **not currently in either paper's bibliography** but appear relevant. Confidence levels indicate how certain we are each reference is real (not hallucinated).

## HIGH PRIORITY — Directly relevant, high confidence

### Attwood articles missing from our bibliographies

1. **Attwood, Jayarava. 2020. "Ungarbling Section VI of the Sanskrit Heart Sutra." *Journal of the Oxford Centre for Buddhist Studies* 18: 11–41.**
   - Shows Sanskrit of Section VI is garbled and syntactically broken, consistent with back-translation from Chinese. Directly relevant to collation work.
   - Confidence: HIGH (found on PhilArchive, Academia.edu)
   - Relevant to: BOTH papers

2. **Attwood, Jayarava. 2020. "Edward Conze: A Re-evaluation of the Man and his Contribution to Prajñāpāramitā Studies." *Journal of the Oxford Centre for Buddhist Studies* 19: 22–51.**
   - Critical reassessment of Conze's scholarship; foundational for Attwood's subsequent revised editions.
   - Confidence: HIGH
   - Relevant to: Both papers (background)

3. **Attwood, Jayarava. 2020. "Studying the Heart Sutra: Basic Sources and Methods (A Response to Ng and Ānando)." *Buddhist Studies Review* 37 (1–2): 199–217.**
   - Methodological article responding to critics of the Chinese-origins thesis.
   - Confidence: HIGH (confirmed via Equinox, PhilArchive, H-Net)
   - Relevant to: Both papers (methodology)

4. **Attwood, Jayarava. 2022. "The Heart Sutra Revisited." *Buddhist Studies Review* 39 (2): 229–254.**
   - Critical review of the five articles in *Acta Asiatica* 121 (Saito, Watanabe, Horiuchi, Ishii, Silk). Key English-language engagement with Japanese scholarship.
   - Confidence: HIGH (confirmed via Equinox, PhilPapers, H-Net)
   - Relevant to: BOTH papers — important for CJK engagement

5. **Attwood, Jayarava. 2022. "The Cessation of Sensory Experience and Prajñāpāramitā Philosophy." *International Journal of Buddhist Thought and Culture* 32 (1): 111–148.**
   - Prajñāpāramitā phenomenology; informs his interpretive framework for the 2024 editions.
   - Confidence: HIGH
   - Relevant to: Parallel compositions (background)

### Scholars arguing against Nattier/Attwood

6. **Harimoto, Kengo. 2021. "Was the Prajñāpāramitāhṛdaya Composed in Sanskrit?" *Journal of Indian and Buddhist Studies* (*Indogaku Bukkyōgaku Kenkyū*) 69 (3): 1038–1044.**
   - Argues for Sanskrit composition, directly countering Chinese-origins thesis. One of the few recent challenges.
   - Confidence: HIGH (confirmed via CiNii Research)
   - Relevant to: BOTH papers — must cite if we engage with origins debate

### Apple's additional Heart Sutra work

7. **Apple, James B. 2020. "On an Indian Source for the Opening Verse of the Tibetan version of the Heart Sūtra." *Annual Report of the International Research Institute for Advanced Buddhology at Soka University* (ARIRIAB) 23.**
   - Argues Atiśa brought the opening verse practice to Tibet from an Indian monastery (late Pāla period). Directly relevant to Tibetan textual tradition.
   - Confidence: HIGH for existence (confirmed on Academia.edu, ResearchGate); page numbers not confirmed.
   - Relevant to: Tibetan edition

### Commentarial studies (Horiuchi on Vimalamitra)

8. **Horiuchi, Toshio. 2022. "Disputed Emptiness: Vimalamitra's Mādhyamika Interpretation of the Heart Sutra in the Light of His Criticism on Other Schools." *Religions* 13 (11): 1067.**
   - Study of Vimalamitra's Heart Sutra commentary — the same figure credited as translator of the Kangyur recension.
   - Confidence: HIGH (MDPI open access)
   - Relevant to: Tibetan edition

9. **Horiuchi, Toshio. 2023. "Madhyamaka vs. Yogācāra: A Previously Unknown Dispute in Vimalamitra's Commentary on the Heart Sutra." *Religions* 14 (3): 327.**
   - Continues Horiuchi's work, revealing previously unknown doctrinal disputes.
   - Confidence: HIGH (MDPI open access)
   - Relevant to: Tibetan edition

### Radich on text classification

10. **Radich, Michael. 2015. "Rethinking the Category of Chinese Buddhist Apocrypha." *Studies in Chinese Religions* 1 (1): 45–73.**
    - Challenges the translation/apocrypha binary; implicitly challenges the chāojīng category. Foundational for text classification arguments.
    - Confidence: HIGH
    - Relevant to: Parallel compositions — cited by Attwood in his feedback (issue A9)

### Willemen on early Heart Sutra history

11. **Willemen, Charles. 2021. "About the Early History of the Heart Sutra." In *Illuminating the Dharma: Buddhist Studies in Honour of Venerable Professor KL Dhammajoti*, 457–466.**
    - On early textual history.
    - Confidence: HIGH (confirmed via Linfield Digital Commons)
    - Relevant to: Both papers

12. **Willemen, Charles. 2023. "Brief Note about the Heart Sutra and its Composition." *The Indian International Journal of Buddhist Studies* 22: 1–15 (approx.).**
    - Addresses composition questions.
    - Confidence: HIGH (confirmed via Linfield Digital Commons)
    - Relevant to: Parallel compositions

## MEDIUM PRIORITY — Methodological parallels, less directly relevant

### Digital editions methodology

13. **Silk, Jonathan A. 2021. "Editing without an Ur-text: Buddhist Sūtras, Rabbinic Text Criticism, and the Open Philology Digital Humanities Project." *Annual Report of the International Research Institute for Advanced Buddhology at Soka University* (ARIRIAB) 24: 147–163.**
    - Fundamental theoretical problem of editing fluid/open texts without a single original. Describes OPEn digital tool. Essential methodology comparison.
    - Confidence: HIGH (PDF on openphilology.eu)
    - Relevant to: Tibetan edition (methodology)

14. **Apple, James B. 2019. "Digital Filiation Studies: Phylogenetic Analysis in the Study of Tibetan Buddhist Canonical Texts." In *Digital Humanities and Buddhism: An Introduction*, ed. Daniel Veidlinger, 209–226. Berlin: De Gruyter.**
    - Computational phylogenetic analysis applied to Kangyur textual criticism.
    - Confidence: HIGH (confirmed via De Gruyter, ResearchGate)
    - Relevant to: Tibetan edition (methodology)

15. **Lin Qian and Michael Radich. 2023. "Notes on the Fo yi jing T793." *Journal of the American Oriental Society* 143 (4): 881–902.**
    - Computational-philological methodology (TACL) for determining text composition. Methodological parallel.
    - Confidence: HIGH
    - Relevant to: Both papers (methodology)

### 84000 translation

16. **84000: Translating the Words of the Buddha. "The Heart of the Perfection of Wisdom, the Blessed Mother" (Toh 21).** https://84000.co/translation/toh21
    - Based on Degé with variant notes from Stog Palace. Not a critical edition per se, but contains collation notes.
    - Confidence: HIGH
    - Relevant to: Tibetan edition

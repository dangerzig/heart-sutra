# Separating Descriptive Findings from Interpretive Claims

## Purpose

This document provides guidance for restructuring the paper so that readers can accept the textual evidence without necessarily accepting the parallel composition interpretation. The peer review (Specific Issue 4) correctly notes that the current draft conflates observations with conclusions.

---

## Descriptive Findings (factual, verifiable)

The following observations are documentable facts about the texts. They do not depend on any interpretive framework:

### D1. Terminology Consistency
T250 consistently uses Kumarajiva-era terminology (觀世音, 五陰, 舍利弗, 菩薩, 般若波羅蜜, 明呪). T251 consistently uses Xuanzang-era terminology (觀自在, 五蘊, 舍利子, 菩提薩埵, 般若波羅蜜多, 咒). There are no exceptions in either direction.

### D2. T250-Unique Passages
T250 contains two passages absent from T251: (a) the skandha-characteristic passage (色空故無惱壞相...) and (b) the temporal negation (是空法，非過去，非未來，非現在). Both passages are doctrinally coherent.

### D3. T223 Extraction Fidelity
In a segment-by-segment comparison, T250 matches T223's wording more closely than T251 does in 8 of 10 sections. T251 matches more closely in 0 sections. The remaining 2 sections are identical across both.

### D4. Epithet Pattern
T250 has three epithets using consistent 明呪 terminology, matching the Large Prajnaparamita pattern (Attwood 2017b). T251 has four epithets with mixed terminology (神咒/明咒/咒), a pattern without precedent in Prajnaparamita literature.

### D5. Shared Identical Core
The five-section negation series (skandhas, ayatanas, dhatus, pratityasamutpada, Four Truths) is word-for-word identical in T250 and T251.

### D6. Ancient Attestation of Divergence
Woncheuk's 7th-century commentary (T1711) already notes variant readings between Heart Sutra versions, including the 苦惱 variant documented in the bodhisattva passage.

## Interpretive Claims (require argument, open to debate)

The following claims are interpretations offered as the best explanation of the descriptive findings. They are provisional and subject to revision:

### I1. Parallel Composition (the hypothesis)
T250 and T251 were composed independently, both extracting from T223, rather than one being a revision of the other. **This is the paper's central interpretive claim.**

### I2. Deletion Implausibility
The absence of T250's unique passages from T251 is better explained by independent composition than by deliberate deletion, because documented 抄經 revision practice does not include deletion of doctrinally correct content. **This is an argument from documented practice, not a logical proof.**

### I3. Forger Sophistication
If T250 is a later fabrication (Attwood's model), its creator would have needed to systematically replace every Xuanzang convention with its Kumarajiva equivalent, add two complete doctrinally appropriate passages, and maintain T223-consistent terminology throughout -- a level of textual sophistication that, while not impossible, deserves explicit consideration. **This is a parsimony argument.**

### I4. Chinese Compositional Priority
Both T250 and T251 were composed in Chinese from T223, not translated from Sanskrit. **This claim depends on Nattier (1992) and is not original to this paper.**

## Recommended Paper Structure

To maintain the distinction between description and interpretation:

### Section 2: Textual Evidence (descriptive)
Present D1-D6 as factual findings with tables and parallel texts. Use language like:
- "T250 reads X where T251 reads Y"
- "This passage is present in T250 and absent from T251"
- "In N of M sections, T250's wording is closer to T223"

Avoid language like:
- "This proves that..."
- "T250 is clearly the earlier text"
- "The reviser would have had to..."

### Section 3: Analysis (transitional)
Discuss what the descriptive findings imply, presenting multiple possible explanations:
- "Three models could account for this pattern: (a) T250 is earlier and T251 revised it, (b) T251 is earlier and T250 was fabricated to restore T223 material, (c) both texts independently extract from T223."
- "We now evaluate each model against the evidence."

### Section 4: Argument for Parallel Composition (interpretive)
Present I1-I3 as argued claims, explicitly framed as interpretation:
- "We propose that parallel composition best accounts for the cumulative evidence..."
- "The revision model (in either direction) faces the following difficulties..."
- "We acknowledge that the fabrication model (Attwood 2020) is not excluded by the evidence, but argue it is less parsimonious because..."

### Section 5: Limitations and Open Questions
- "The parallel composition hypothesis is offered as a provisional model, not a definitive conclusion."
- "The following evidence would challenge this interpretation: [see FALSIFIABILITY.md]"
- "We have not been able to consult [Fukui 1987, INBUDS, CNKI] exhaustively."

## Key Phrases for the Paper

**Descriptive (safe):**
- "The textual evidence shows that..."
- "T250 preserves / T251 modifies..."
- "This pattern is consistent across all examined sections"
- "Woncheuk's commentary attests to this divergence in the 7th century"

**Interpretive (flag clearly):**
- "We interpret this pattern as evidence for..."
- "The most parsimonious explanation, in our view, is..."
- "This is more difficult to explain under the revision model because..."
- "We propose, provisionally, that..."

**To avoid:**
- "This proves..." (nothing in textual criticism is proven)
- "Clearly..." (masks the interpretive step)
- "It is evident that..." (same problem)
- "The only explanation is..." (there are always alternatives)

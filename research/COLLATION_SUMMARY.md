## Key Findings

### 1. T250 Contains Unique Material

T250 has two substantial passages absent from T251 and all Sanskrit/Tibetan witnesses:

**Skandha Characteristics (T250:2a)**
> 色空故無惱壞相，受空故無受相，想空故無知相，行空故無作相，識空故無覺相。

**Temporal Negation (T250:3a)**
> 是空法，非過去，非未來，非現在。

These passages represent doctrinal elaboration, not padding. A reviser creating T251 from T250 would have no obvious reason to delete them.

### 2. T250 Matches Large Sūtra More Closely

| Feature | T250 | T251 | T223 (Large Sūtra) |
|---------|------|------|---------------------|
| Epithets | 3 (all with 明呪) | 4 (mixed) | 3 (all with 明呪) |
| pāramitā | 波羅蜜 | 波羅蜜多 | 波羅蜜 |
| Avalokiteśvara | 觀世音 | 觀自在 | 觀世音 |
| Skandhas | 五陰 | 五蘊 | 五陰 |

T250's exact match with T223 patterns suggests direct extraction; T251's innovations suggest independent composition or deliberate update.

### 3. Sanskrit Shows Back-Translation Evidence

| Feature | Evidence |
|---------|----------|
| kṣaya vs nirodha | Sanskrit uses kṣaya (destruction) where all Prajñāpāramitā literature uses nirodha (cessation). Chinese 盡 is ambiguous; back-translator chose wrong term. |
| rūpān na pṛthak | Unusual ablative construction; calque from Chinese 色不異空. |
| yad rūpaṃ sā śūnyatā | Line present in all Sanskrit, absent from all Chinese. Later expansion. |

### 4. Late Interpolations Identified

| Interpolation | Present In | Absent From |
|---------------|------------|-------------|
| oṃ in mantra | Nb, Ne, T257 | T251, T250, Fangshan, T256, Ja, Na |

| Elaborate maṅgala | Late Nepalese | All early witnesses |

## Implications for Scholarship

### For Chinese Composition Thesis (Nattier 1992)
- Confirmed: kṣaya/nirodha, grammatical calques, T223 parallels
- Extended: systematic documentation across all witnesses

### For T250/T251 Relationship (Original Finding)
- T250 and T251 show consistent but mutually incompatible terminology
- T250 preserves material absent from T251 and all derived traditions
- T250 matches Large Sūtra patterns more closely
- Best explanation: parallel compositions, not successive revisions

### For Critical Edition Practice
- Conze's edition privileged late Nepalese manuscripts incorrectly
- Hōryūji manuscript is key early Sanskrit witness (lacks oṃ, na-aprāptiḥ)
- Direction-of-dependence must be marked in apparatus

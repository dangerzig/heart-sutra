# Prajñāpāramitāhṛdaya Synoptic Alignment

## Methodology

- **Base text**: T251 (Chinese)
- **Sanskrit witness**: GRETIL
- **Tibetan witness**: Toh21

---

## Opening

### T251:1

**Chinese (Base)**
> 觀自在菩薩，行深般若波羅蜜多時，照見五蘊皆空，度一切苦厄。

*Pinyin*: Guānzìzài púsà, xíng shēn bōrě bōluómìduō shí, zhàojiàn wǔyùn jiē kōng, dù yīqiè kǔ è.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Ārya-avalokiteśvaro bodhisattvo gambhīrāṃ prajñāpāramitācaryāṃ caramāṇo vyavalokayati sma: pañca-skandhās, tāṃś ca svabhāva-śūnyān paśyati sma.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> आर्य-अवलोकितेश्वरो बोधिसत्त्वो गम्भीरां प्रज्ञापारमिताचर्यां चरमाणो व्यवलोकयति स्म: पञ्च-स्कन्धास्, तांश्च स्वभाव-शून्यान् पश्यति स्म।

**English Gloss**
> When Avalokiteśvara Bodhisattva was practicing the profound prajñāpāramitā, he illuminated the five aggregates and saw that they are all empty, thus transcending all suffering.

**Notes on Divergences**
- Sanskrit: vyavalokayati sma - imperfect tense unusual for sūtra style

## Form Emptiness

### T251:2

**Chinese (Base)**
> 舍利子，色不異空，空不異色；色即是空，空即是色。受想行識亦復如是。

*Pinyin*: Shèlìzǐ, sè bù yì kōng, kōng bù yì sè; sè jí shì kōng, kōng jí shì sè. Shòu xiǎng xíng shí yì fù rúshì.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Iha Śāriputra rūpaṃ śūnyatā śūnyataiva rūpam, rūpān na pṛthak śūnyatā śūnyatāyā na pṛthag rūpam, yad rūpaṃ sā śūnyatā yā śūnyatā tad rūpam; evam eva vedanā-saṃjñā-saṃskāra-vijñānāni.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> इह शारिपुत्र रूपं शून्यता शून्यतैव रूपम्, रूपान् न पृथक् शून्यता शून्यतायाः न पृथग् रूपम्, यद् रूपं सा शून्यता या शून्यता तद् रूपम्; एवमेव वेदना-संज्ञा-संस्कार-विज्ञानानि।

**Tibetan**
> གཟུགས་སྟོང་པའོ། སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་གཟུགས་སོ། གཟུགས་ལས་སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་གཞན་མ་ཡིན། སྟོང་པ་ཉིད་ལས་ཀྱང་གཟུགས་གཞན་མ་ཡིན་ནོ།

*Wylie*: gzugs stong pa'o/ stong pa nyid gzugs so/ gzugs las stong pa nyid gzhan ma yin/ stong pa nyid las kyang gzugs gzhan ma yin no/

**English Gloss**
> Śāriputra, form is not different from emptiness, emptiness is not different from form; form is emptiness, emptiness is form. Feeling, perception, formation, and consciousness are also like this.

## Characteristics

### T251:3

**Chinese (Base)**
> 舍利子，是諸法空相，不生不滅，不垢不淨，不增不減。

*Pinyin*: Shèlìzǐ, shì zhūfǎ kōng xiāng, bù shēng bù miè, bù gòu bù jìng, bù zēng bù jiǎn.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Iha Śāriputra sarva-dharmāḥ śūnyatā-lakṣaṇā, anutpannā aniruddhā, amalā avimalā, anūnā aparipūrṇāḥ.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> इह शारिपुत्र सर्व-धर्माः शून्यता-लक्षणा, अनुत्पन्ना अनिरुद्धा, अमला अविमला, अनूना अपरिपूर्णाः।

**English Gloss**
> Śāriputra, the emptiness characteristic of all dharmas is: not arising, not ceasing; not defiled, not pure; not increasing, not decreasing.

## Negations Skandhas

### T251:4

**Chinese (Base)**
> 是故空中，無色，無受想行識，

*Pinyin*: Shìgù kōng zhōng, wú sè, wú shòu xiǎng xíng shí,

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Tasmāc Chāriputra śūnyatāyāṃ na rūpaṃ na vedanā na saṃjñā na saṃskārāḥ na vijñānam.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> तस्माच् छारिपुत्र शून्यतायां न रूपं न वेदना न संज्ञा न संस्काराः न विज्ञानम्।

**English Gloss**
> Therefore, in emptiness there is no form, no feeling, perception, formation, or consciousness;

## Negations Ayatanas

**Sanskrit (IAST)**

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> न चक्षुः-श्रोत्र-घ्राण-जिह्वा-काय-मनांसि। न रूप-शब्द-गन्ध-रस-स्प्रष्टव्य-धर्माः।

**English Gloss**
> no eyes, ears, nose, tongue, body, or mind; no forms, sounds, smells, tastes, tangibles, or mental objects;

## Negations Pratityasamutpada

### T251:7

**Chinese (Base)**
> 無無明，亦無無明盡，乃至無老死，亦無老死盡。

*Pinyin*: Wú wúmíng, yì wú wúmíng jìn, nǎizhì wú lǎosǐ, yì wú lǎosǐ jìn.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Na-avidyā na-avidyā-kṣayo yāvan na jarā-maraṇaṃ na jarā-maraṇa-kṣayo.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> न-अविद्या न-अविद्या-क्षयो यावन् न जरा-मरणं न जरा-मरण-क्षयो।

**English Gloss**
> No ignorance and no ending of ignorance, up to and including no old age and death, and no ending of old age and death.

**Notes on Divergences**
- Sanskrit: Uses kṣaya (destruction) instead of standard nirodha (cessation) - evidence of back-translation (Nattier 1992)

## Negations Truths

### T251:8

**Chinese (Base)**
> 無苦集滅道，無智亦無得，以無所得故。

*Pinyin*: Wú kǔ jí miè dào, wú zhì yì wú dé, yǐ wú suǒ dé gù.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Na duḥkha-samudaya-nirodha-mārgā. Na jñānaṃ, na prāptir na-aprāptiḥ.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> न दुःख-समुदय-निरोध-मार्गा। न ज्ञानं, न प्राप्तिर् न-अप्राप्तिः।

**English Gloss**
> No suffering, origination, cessation, or path; no wisdom and no attainment, because there is nothing to attain.

## Bodhisattva Result

### T251:9

**Chinese (Base)**
> 菩提薩埵，依般若波羅蜜多故。心無罣礙，無罣礙故。無有恐怖，遠離顚倒夢想，究竟涅槃。

*Pinyin*: Pútísàduǒ, yī bōrě bōluómìduō gù. Xīn wú guà'ài, wú guà'ài gù. Wú yǒu kǒngbù, yuǎnlí diāndǎo mèngxiǎng, jiūjìng nièpán.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Tasmāc Chāriputra aprāptitvād bodhisattvasya prajñāpāramitām āśritya viharaty acittāvaraṇaḥ. Cittāvaraṇa-nāstitvād atrastro viparyāsa-atikrānto niṣṭhā-nirvāṇa-prāptaḥ.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> तस्माच् छारिपुत्र अप्राप्तित्वाद् बोधिसत्त्वस्य प्रज्ञापारमितामाश्रित्य विहरत्यचित्तावरणः। चित्तावरण-नास्तित्वाद् अत्रस्त्रो विपर्यास-अतिक्रान्तो निष्ठा-निर्वाण-प्राप्तः।

**English Gloss**
> Because bodhisattvas rely on prajñāpāramitā, their minds are without obstruction. Without obstruction, they have no fear, are far removed from inverted views and dreams, and attain ultimate nirvāṇa.

**Notes on Divergences**
- Sanskrit: niṣṭhā-nirvāṇa controversial reading (Attwood 2018)

## Buddha Result

### T251:10

**Chinese (Base)**
> 三世諸佛，依般若波羅蜜多故，得阿耨多羅三藐三菩提。

*Pinyin*: Sānshì zhū fó, yī bōrě bōluómìduō gù, dé ānòuduōluó sānmiǎo sānpútí.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Tryadhva-vyavasthitāḥ sarva-buddhāḥ prajñāpāramitām āśrityānuttarāṃ samyak-saṃbodhim abhisaṃbuddhāḥ.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> त्र्यध्व-व्यवस्थिताः सर्व-बुद्धाः प्रज्ञापारमितामाश्रित्यानुत्तरां सम्यक्-संबोधिमभिसंबुद्धाः।

**English Gloss**
> All buddhas of the three times, relying on prajñāpāramitā, attain anuttarā-samyak-saṃbodhi.

## Mantra Praise

### T251:11

**Chinese (Base)**
> 故知般若波羅蜜多，是大神咒，是大明咒，是無上咒，是無等等咒，能除一切苦，眞實不虛故。

*Pinyin*: Gùzhī bōrě bōluómìduō, shì dà shén zhòu, shì dà míng zhòu, shì wúshàng zhòu, shì wú děngděng zhòu, néng chú yīqiè kǔ, zhēnshí bù xū gù.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Tasmāj jñātavyam: prajñāpāramitā mahā-mantro mahā-vidyā-mantro 'nuttara-mantro 'samasama-mantraḥ, sarva-duḥkha-praśamanaḥ, satyam amithyatvāt.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> तस्माज् ज्ञातव्यम्: प्रज्ञापारमिता महा-मन्त्रो महा-विद्या-मन्त्रो ऽनुत्तर-मन्त्रो ऽसमसम-मन्त्रः, सर्व-दुःख-प्रशमनः, सत्यमम् इथ्यत्वात्।

**English Gloss**
> Therefore know that prajñāpāramitā is the great mantra, the mantra of great illumination, the supreme mantra, the unequalled mantra, which can remove all suffering; it is true and not false.

## Mantra

### T251:12

**Chinese (Base)**
> 說般若波羅蜜多咒，卽說咒曰：揭帝揭帝，波羅揭帝，波羅僧揭帝，菩提僧莎訶。

*Pinyin*: Shuō bōrě bōluómìduō zhòu, jí shuō zhòu yuē: Jiēdì jiēdì, bōluó jiēdì, bōluó sēng jiēdì, pútí sēng suōhē.

**Sanskrit (IAST)**
> Prajñāpāramitāyām ukto mantraḥ. Tadyathā: Oṃ gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā.

**Sanskrit (Devanagari)**
> प्रज्ञापारमितायामुक्तो मन्त्रः। तद्यथा: ॐ गते गते पारगते पारसंगते बोधि स्वाहा।

**Tibetan**
> ཏདྱ་ཐཱ། ཨོཾ་ག་ཏེ་ག་ཏེ་པཱ་ར་ག་ཏེ་པཱ་ར་སཾ་ག་ཏེ་བོ་དྷི་སྭཱ་ཧཱ།

*Wylie*: tad+ya thA/ oM ga te ga te pA ra ga te pA ra saM ga te bo d+hi swA hA/

**English Gloss**
> Therefore proclaim the prajñāpāramitā mantra, proclaim the mantra that says: Gate gate pāragate pārasaṃgate bodhi svāhā.
